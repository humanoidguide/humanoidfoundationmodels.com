FAQ

Questions, answered.

What exactly is a humanoid world model?
A foundation model that lets a robot simulate the outcome of an action before it takes it — the emerging alternative to today’s vision-language-action (VLA) models. Mapping that transition is the heart of the report.
Who is the report for?
Investors and strategy teams, robotics and AI engineers, foundation-model builders, and the OEMs and suppliers choosing a ‘brain’ for their platforms.
How does this differ from the Market Report and the Supply Chain report?
This one covers the intelligence layer — world models, VLAs, architectures and the people building them, plus the 40-model Brain Score directory. The Market Report takes the broad market view, and the Supply Chain report handles components and suppliers.
What is the Brain Score?
Our editorial 0–10 score for every model across ten capabilities, from locomotion and whole-body control through real-time inference and long-horizon planning.
What is the methodology?
Published results, benchmarks and primary industry sources, synthesized with full citations; the Brain Scores themselves are humanoid.guide’s editorial judgment.
